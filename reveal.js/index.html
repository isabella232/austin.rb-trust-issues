Trust In (Meatspace) Society

Enlightenment Thought

The birth of Social Theory

  • They wanted to know the axiomatic basis for society
  • They came up with some notions held to be universal

Mankind without socieity exists in a 'state of nature'

What Hobbes famously described as 'nasty, brutish, and short'

  • Everyone out for themselves
  • No incentives to cooperate

Social contract

The notion that we give up some liberties in order to have some level of defined order/safety/security provided by a ruler or legal framework.

  • Somehow, we live in societies - not a state of nature. How does this work?
  • Big notion - pretty much all the hot thinkers had their version
  • we give up some freedoms in order to have some guarantees
  • this is true in every kind of society
  • there must be axiomatic notions in sociology, much like math

Their conclusions?

Humans are incentivized to behave

These incentives form the basis of societal pressures codified in law:

  • morality
  • reputation
  • cooperation
  • Everything flows from these frameworks
  • Morals: Don't kill because it's wrong (religious: harmful to soul/karma)
  • Rep: Don't kill because you'd be ostracized
  • Coop: Don't kill because he can help me
Act in such a way that you treat humanity, whether in your own person or in the person of any other, never merely as a means to an end, but always at the same time as an end.
- Immanuel Kant, Grounding for the Metaphysics of Morals
  • This is pretty much the Golden Rule
  • But Kant is also saying in his way that we are incentivized to do this
  • He calls this the 'Categorical Imperative' and in his view it's the basis of society

Motivations and Morals

  • political philosophy studies the roots of societal interactions
  • psychology tells us about the individual's motivations
  • sociology studies collective human behavior
  • So over time we develop a set of ways to study this stuff
  • We learn more about human behavior at the individual and collective level

Is that it?

And we now know that at a certain point, someone will defect

What is defection?

Basically it's when a human actor decides to act against collective interest and in favor of individual interest

So what?

On the Internet, defection happens quickly

  • Social contract doesn't exist
  • Meatspace constraints on behavior don't exist
  • There are no real consequences for acting like a sociopath

Trust on the Internet

Without a social contract, how do we avoid trust on the Internet?

We don't

We will never have total control over our societal environment on the Internet - same as in Real Life

At some point, trust is inevitable

But you shouldn't trust more than you need to

People find this idea depressing sometimes

Because they make the mistake of equating the Internet with human society we encounter IRL

But they shouldn't

The Internet is not the same as Meatspace society and can't be treated as such

What are our tools in a diminished-trust environment?

  • Encryption
  • Authentication
  • Vigilance
  • Faith

Yeah, faith

At a certain point, you have no choice but to depend on others - even on the Internet.

In Meatspace, you might go to a movie, trusting that you won't be shot. People have been shot at the movies before, but you have faith that it will be safe for you.

There are times when similar inevitable trust calculations are made on the Internet

But you are probably trusting more things than you think

Knowing *when* you are relying on faith is paramount

1.4.1. Appropriate Certificate Uses

Certificates issued under this CP may be used for the purposes designated in the key usage and extended key usage fields found in the certificate. However, the sensitivity of the information processed or protected by a certificate varies greatly, and each Relying Party must evaluate the application environment and associated risks before deciding on whether to use a certificate issued under this CP.

1.4.2. Prohibited Certificate Uses

Certificates do not guarantee that the Subject is trustworthy, honest, reputable in its business dealings, compliant with any laws, or safe to do business with. A certificate only establishes that the information in the certificate was verified as reasonably correct when the certificate issued. Code signing certificates do not indicate that the signed code is safe to install or is free from malware, bugs, or vulnerabilities.

Hotlinking JS libraries

            <script src='http://code.jquery.com/jquery-2.1.1.js' />
  • ASK AUDIENCE: how many of you work on web apps and do this kind of thing?
  • Hotlinking JS libraries can be risky. Doing it without SSL is irresponsible. jquery gets pwned and you're in bad shape
  • jQuery has been pwned before for this exact reason. Literally thousands of sites could have backdoors injected this way
  • Kinda bad to do this at all, but if you're going to, at least use SSL to get it

Conclusions

For hundreds of years, we've known how human societies work

We know what human societies in Meatspace rely on

The Internet lacks the same constraints as Meatspace

There's no similar social order, so trust makes less sense

Whether as a dev or end user, know what you're trusting

Minimize the trust wherever you can

Don't think of security as 'what to be afraid of'

(there are too many things to be afraid of)

Think of it as 'how to sell a better trust product'
