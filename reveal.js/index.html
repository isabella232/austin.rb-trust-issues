Trust on the Internet

Without a social contract, how do we avoid trust on the Internet?

We don't

We will never have total control over our societal environment on the Internet - same as in Real Life

At some point, trust is inevitable

But you shouldn't trust more than you need to

People find this idea depressing sometimes

Because they make the mistake of equating the Internet with human society we encounter IRL

But they shouldn't

The Internet is not the same as Meatspace society and can't be treated as such

What are our tools in a diminished-trust environment?

  • Encryption
  • Authentication
  • Vigilance
  • Faith

Yeah, faith

At a certain point, you have no choice but to depend on others - even on the Internet.

In Meatspace, you might go to a movie, trusting that you won't be shot. People have been shot at the movies before, but you have faith that it will be safe for you.

There are times when similar inevitable trust calculations are made on the Internet

But you are probably trusting more things than you think

Knowing *when* you are relying on faith is paramount

Selling Trust

Trust is your metaproduct as a software developer

In some cases, we are literally
dealing with life and death

  • medical devices
  • car/train/plane firmware
  • Industrial Control Systems(controlling powerplants, factories, pipelines, etc)

But no matter what you get paid for, you are selling trust

We are all charged with the stewardship of data:

  • privacy
  • accuracy
  • security

Things we can control,
things we must advocate for

Under our control

  • where JS libraries come from
  • whether we encrypt the transport layer
  • whether we look for problems in our code
  • Hard to think of places where SSL *shouldn't* be
  • Static analysis tools can help with this. Breakman is free. CodeClimate is worth it.

Hotlinking JS libraries

            <script src='http://code.jquery.com/jquery-2.1.1.js' />
  • ASK AUDIENCE: how many of you work on web apps and do this kind of thing?
  • Hotlinking JS libraries can be risky. Doing it without SSL is irresponsible. jquery gets pwned and you're in bad shape
  • jQuery has been pwned before for this exact reason. Literally thousands of sites could have backdoors injected this way
  • Kinda bad to do this at all, but if you're going to, at least use SSL to get it

Consider SSL everywhere

Requiring our Advocacy

OpenSSL

  • be aware of what you are using
  • gain some understanding of ciphers, encryption handshakes, etc
  • realize that OpenSSL is pretty strange and fragile, despite its ubiquity
  • I would wager no one in this room is qualified to commit on OpenSSL. I'm certainly not
  • this stuff is extremely hard
  • What encryption suites you are using/supporting matter quite a bit
  • What version of SSL/TLS you are using is important

Package Managers Should Give a Sh*t At All (NPM)

  • NPM doesn't even offer a *checksum* of its tarball, much less a signed binary
  • NPM installs over HTTP - not over SSL.
  • This is easy to fix. WTF, NPM?

No biggie tho, NPM just installs everything

  • Just doing this talk required me installing the socket.io package - what could go wrong?
  • People relying on a Grunt file and just 'npm install' are trusting too many
