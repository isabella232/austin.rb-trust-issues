Game theory has a lot to say about this too

We can do experiments

And we now know that at a certain point, someone will defect

What is defection?

Basically it's when a human actor decides to act against collective interest and in favor of individual interest

So what?

On the Internet, defection happens quickly

  • Social contract doesn't exist
  • Meatspace constraints on behavior don't exist
  • There are no real consequences for acting like a sociopath

The Internet is full of thieves, trolls and perverts

Without a social contract, how do we avoid trust on the Internet?

We don't

We will never have total control over your societal environment on the Internet - same as in Real Life

At some point, trust is inevitable

But you shouldn't trust more than you need to

People find this idea depressing sometimes

Because they make the mistake of equating the Internet with human society we encounter IRL

But they shouldn't

The Internet is not the same as Meatspace society and can't be treated as such

What are our tools in a diminished-trust environment?

  • Encryption
  • Authentication
  • Vigilance
  • Faith

Yeah, faith

At a certain point, you have no choice but to depend on others - even on the Internet.

In Meatspace, you might go to a movie, trusting that you won't be shot. People have been shot at the movies before, but you have faith that it will be safe for you.

There are times when similar inevitable trust calculations are made on the Internet

But you are probably trusting more things than you think

Knowing *when* you are relying on faith is paramount
